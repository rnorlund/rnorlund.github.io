Research

My lab uses state-of-the-art MRI and machine learning to understand how the brain changes across the lifespan — and what those changes mean for language, memory, and recovery after stroke. We're particularly interested in how cardiovascular health, white matter integrity, and brain age relate to cognitive function.

Key Themes

🧓

Brain Aging & the Lifespan

Through the Aging Brain Cohort (ABC) Study, we track how brain structure and function change across the adult lifespan using multimodal MRI, genetics, cognition, and behavioral data. We've shown that advanced brain age predicts worse outcomes in aphasia, and that socioeconomic status and cardiovascular health both influence brain aging trajectories.

💬

Aphasia & Stroke Recovery

As part of C-STAR, we investigate how people recover language abilities after stroke. Our work uses deep learning to predict aphasia severity from brain morphometry patterns, and examines how white matter hyperintensities and lesion expansion affect long-term language outcomes. We've released the Aphasia Recovery Cohort as an open-source resource.

🤖

Machine Learning & Neuroimaging

We apply deep learning and ML pipelines to neuroimaging data for brain age estimation, lesion-symptom mapping, and outcome prediction. Our tools include Brainchop (edge AI for neuroimaging), NiiVue (web-based visualization), and ML-based multimodal prediction models for language recovery.

🪞

Mirror Neurons & Joint Action

My earlier work established that the mirror neuron system is more active during complementary actions than imitative ones (Nature Neuroscience, 2007). This line of research explores the neural basis of cooperation, social cognition, and how we coordinate actions with others.

⚡

Brain Stimulation

We use transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS) and transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS/theta-burst) to modulate brain function — investigating effects on motor learning, inhibitory control in addiction, and rehabilitation outcomes in stroke and TBI patients.

🧬

Alzheimer's & Cardiovascular Health

As a core investigator at SC-ADRC, we study how cardiovascular risk factors, white matter disease, and genetic markers (APOE4, FOXP2) relate to cognitive decline. Recent work links periodontal health, dietary fiber intake, and pulse pressure to brain structure and cognition.
